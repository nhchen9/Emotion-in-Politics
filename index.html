Words are powerful.


Skilled orators craft speech into precise argumentation, diction can build power or undermine credibility, and debate influences thought and action.

And, especially in a democracy, speech directs the course of entire nations - for better or worse

Motivation

According to former United States Attorney Anton Valukas, one of the most pressing societal issues the US faces is a lack of substantive political dialogue. Political debates between constituents and candidates alike have grown increasingly adversarial in recent years, and often devolve into personal attacks rather than an attempt to address real world concerns. Valukas maintains that one of the White House's most important roles is to foster healthy, constructive political discussion - and it's not doing a great job. In most recent presidential election, presidential candidates from both sides, potential leaders of the free world, habitually resorted to grade school tactics of name-calling and slander.

This trend of irrational pathos in the place of logos - actual problem solving - detracts from the government's effectiveness solving key societal issues of justice, opportunity, and individual rights. We've seen this time and time again in studying mass incarceration. Hatred and insecurity among poor whites feeds the flames of racism, segregation and violence in the New Jim Crow. Irrational thirst for vengeance ruins communities through unjust imprisonment and execution in Just Mercy. Fear pushes people to imprison children, locking demographics into perpetual continuities of inequality in the school to prison pipeline.

For these reasons, I decided to investigate the effects of emotion in politics.

My Method

In order to conduct any sort of analysis for this study, I needed a tool to calculate polarity of emotion in speech, and to pick a set of consistent speeches to analyze. I employed a machine learning sentiment analyzer from the Natural Language Toolkit, and ran it on each sentence in the last 15 presidential inauguration speeches4 and took the average. Inauguration speeches were chosen because they all serve a similar purpose, implying a helpful standardization of expected diction. The machine learning sentiment analyzer provides an index of sentiment polarity - basically how intense of an emotion was used in the text. I then plotted my findings against 4 year change in RGDP, annual change in homicide rate, and 4 year change in unemployment, and performed linear regression with basic statistical analysis on each correlation.

Note that this is a research study, not an experiment. These findings are not intended to establish a causal relationship, it merely inspects correlations and extrapolates potential explanations based on historical evidence.

Analysis

The data demonstrates a strong relationship between change in unemployment rates and emotional intensity, and a moderate relationship between RGDP and homicide rates with emotional intensity. I hypothesize that this relation occurs because, as Attorney Valukas suggested, the White House sets an example for political discourse throughout the nation. Much like toddlers emulate observed behaviors from their peers and parents, US citizens learn from the president and his aides. An excessively emotional and subjective approach to problem solving ultimately serves as a distraction, and leads to personal investment in ideally objective positions.

Solution

Democracy's greatest merit lies within its founding ideology: a government for the people, by the people. At the end of the day, individual citizens have the power to influence policy through discussion, debate, and voting. I encourage you to use logic and rationality when discussing governmental policy and political candidates because it sets a positive example for both your peers and representatives. When voting, consider the merits of actual policies each candidate sponsors, rather than just getting swept up by rhetoric. In doing so, we can help guide our government to improve the world.
